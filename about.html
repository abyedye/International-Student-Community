Story from members

Our Story

About 90% who comes to us as an international student or as a green card holder gets victimized of the scam. Basically, the scam is like a phone call which asks you for your information and money by telling that the victim that they have won the money price or sometimes with the threat of getting kicked out of the country.

It is obvious the place is new and we get scared at first as we come to the land of opportinities and the only hope we have is to make our future better. We think what happens if we are let go from this country thats the trick scammer use and we can easily fall under it. Anytime you get call and don't have any idea on what to do, contact us or the one who you can trust around you. We are here to help you and never ever let scammer take benefit from you.

Soccer club

We have a group called summer futbol where we have team from nigeria, nepal, china and so on. The group was originally started by the man himself Suman Baniya. He has been recruiting good players for the competition.

Anyone can join the club and be part of it. You dont have to be pro to join it, everyone here is learning. Basically the main theme is to build a good communication and contact.

ISO Picnic

Once in a year or sometimes twice a year we ISO memeber organize picninc. The picnic is basically a potluck. Everyone who is member brings up the food of their culture and we try it. The picnic usually is organized in independence park.

Each year numbers are increasing and we get chance to try many varities of cultural food. In the mean time we also get to know eachother clearly and get contact information so we can keep in touch. The picnic is fun and always memorable.

Fresh Check Day

SMSU hosted its first Fresh Check Day, a mental health and wellness-like fair, on Wednesday, Nov. 2 in the upper level of the Student Center. Hosted by Counseling and Testing Services with assistance from on- and off-campus resources, the event featured approximately 20 interactive booths and activities dealing in a positive, upbeat way with mental health and wellness. The event was the first Fresh Check Day event in the Midwest, said Dr. Sara Fier, one of the event coordinators who works in the Counseling and Testing Services office. It was hosted with support from the Jordan Porco Foundation.
